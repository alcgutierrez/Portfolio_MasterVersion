About

I live to create and bring something to life. For most of my childhood and adolescence, my creative pursuits have led me to drawing on the walls of my house (then eventually on paper), performing onstage (my greatest feat to date, in this regard, was performing in a musical as the lead character for almost six years) and to writing songs and making music.

However, there was still something missing in my pursuit. As someone who has always been inclined to the arts, I longed to do something creative in the scientific/technical fields.



And that’s when I met programming and web design.

It was pretty much love at first sight—web design/UX design is a niche where my creative and structured mind is able to flourish. My drive to learn has led me to taking self-paced courses online and, when not working on other people's projects, I work on my own passion projects (like this site)!

The pursuit of creativity in the sciences has led me to an interesting place—I am currently in my third year as a Combined Major in Science (Computer Science, Microbiology+Immunology, and Earth Science) student at the University of British Columbia. Everyday, I'm constantly learning that even microscopic, intracellular processes are quite unique and inventive—a conversation topic for another day!

Although my path of creative pursuit has taken different turns, there has been one common theme: there is inspiration in every corner.

Through every encounter, I am inspired to create innovative solutions to the growing needs of the world.

Got an inspiring idea to help make the world a better place through sciences/tech/arts (or all three)? Let's talk!
